Tony Romo’s career, visualized

By John Hancock | Interactives Editor

Published Mar. 6, 2017

    It will be fitting if Tony Romo’s final pass as a Dallas Cowboy is a touchdown, specifically a 3-yard strike to Terrance Williams in the final game of the 2016 season. Despite that final score by Romo, it will be a whisper of an ending for his Cowboys career, which began in earnest Week 7 of the 2006 season when he took over for a struggling Drew Bledsoe. Romo holds the franchise record for regular season passing yards (34,183) and passing touchdowns (248), and ranks second to Troy Aikman in regular season attempts (4,335) and completions (2,829).

    And every receiver on the other end

    Someone had to catch all those passes. Most of the time it was Jason Witten, who accounts for more than 23 percent of Romo’s completions. Dez Bryant and Miles Austin were Romo’s next favorite targets, and along with Witten the trio caught more than 40 percent of Romo’s completions. Even Romo caught a pass from himself, though it went for a 1-yard loss. (Note: In some instances players gained or lost yardage on lateral plays where they were not the initial receiver or because of a fumble.)
